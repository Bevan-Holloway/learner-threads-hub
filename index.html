Being a good learner has nothing to do with IQ.
It means engaging intelligently with things that are complicated, confusing, and challenging in order to grow ever more competent. These seven dispositions allow all learners to stretch their intelligence beyond what they thought possible.

Introducing the Magnificent Seven
The Magnificent Seven is a dispositional framework developed by Bevan Holloway, Guy Claxton, and Becky Carlzon. It is designed to help learners get better at imagining or collaborating, etc, in any subject, and get better at spotting opportunities to engage certain dispositions.

🔍 Curiosity is our engine of learning

Curious learners have minds full of questions, enjoy exploring new things, and relish being amazed. They may question things that are "taken for granted."

💪 Persistence helps us stay engaged

Persistent learners stay intelligently engaged when things get hard. They're willing to risk, put in practice time, and aren't afraid of difficulty or complexity.

💭 Imagination is where we play with ideas

Imaginative learners are inventive risk-takers who see associations others miss. They make good use of metaphors and enjoy day-dreaming about possibilities.

🎯 Attention focuses us on what matters

Attentive learners can prioritise and concentrate well. They're observant, follow interests, and have strategies that help them focus without getting hijacked by distractions.

🧠 Thinking is how we sharpen ideas

Thinking learners like to "hook things up" and think things through carefully. They're committed to understanding deeply and testing ideas, even when challenging.

🤝 Communication & Collaboration connect us to others

Collaborative learners can take on varying group roles, build on others' contributions, and communicate clearly. They understand others and use feedback effectively.

⭐ Craftsmanship drives us toward mastery

Caring learners have an ethic of excellence. They have a nose for quality, work on improving products, and know the importance of reviewing and revising.

Head, Heart, Hand:
Each disposition can be expressed through thinking (Head), emotion (Heart), and action (Hand), creating rich learning experiences across all subjects. The Navigator helps students access these different 'expressions', while Reflections helps them recognise and celebrate when they've used these dispositions.

🔗 How They Work Together

Students use the Navigator to develop their learning powers, then reflect on that growth using the Reflections tool. This creates a powerful cycle of action, reflection, and development that builds true learning expertise.

For Teachers: Both tools provide rich insights into student thinking and create opportunities for meaningful learning conversations.
For Students: They develop genuine learning agency and the vocabulary to articulate their own growth.
